The data reveals two different goalkeepers. In the Premier League, behind a struggling defence, his metrics plummeted. In the Championship, as part of a dominant team, he produced a historically elite season, breaking the league record for clean sheets. This suggests he is a "front-foot" goalkeeper who thrives on control, not a "siege" keeper who excels under constant pressure.

Career Trajectory

From City's academy to record-breaker, Trafford's path has been one of peaks and troughs.

Man City Academy (2015-2021)

Forged in a possession-based philosophy, learning the fundamentals of a modern, ball-playing goalkeeper.

Accrington Stanley (2021)

A harsh introduction to senior football. Conceded 2.36 goals per 90 in a difficult but crucial learning experience.

Bolton Wanderers (2022-23)

A transformative spell. Set a club record with 26 clean sheets in one season, establishing himself as a top EFL keeper.

Burnley - Premier League (2023-24)

A "baptism of fire." Struggled in a relegated side and was eventually dropped, highlighting key areas for development.

Burnley - Championship (2024-25)

A phenomenal redemption. Broke the Championship clean sheet record (29) and re-established his elite potential.

Tale of the Tape: Strengths vs. Weaknesses

A granular look at the attributes that define Trafford's game, revealing both his high ceiling and his critical development areas.

Strengths

Shot-Stopping

His greatest asset. Combines a 6'6" frame with explosive agility. His Championship season saw him prevent nearly 13 goals more than expected.

Mental Fortitude

Supreme self-confidence and proven resilience, bouncing back from major setbacks at Accrington and in the Premier League to produce record-breaking form.

Weaknesses

Distribution Under Pressure

While competent with short passes, his long-range accuracy is a liability and his decision-making falters against an intense, coordinated press.

Aerial Dominance

A key area of concern in the Premier League. He struggled with the physicality and quality of crosses, logging very few successful high claims.

Tactical Showdown: City's Goalkeeping DNA

How does Trafford's statistical profile stack up against Manchester City's current options, Ederson and Stefan Ortega?

The radar chart reveals distinct profiles. Ederson is the revolutionary distributor. Ortega is the reliable, elite shot-stopper. Trafford's profile, based on his best season, shows potential to match Ortega's shot-stopping but significantly lags Ederson's offensive contribution via long passing and aggressive sweeping.

The Verdict

Acquiring Trafford would signal a move towards the "Ortega model" (prioritizing saves) and away from the unique "Ederson model" (goalkeeper as an attacking catalyst).

Unlike his successful predecessors who proved their individual quality in the top flight (even in relegated teams), Trafford's first attempt ended with him being dropped. This makes his potential leap to City a higher-risk proposition.

Final Recommendation

The "Competitive Apprentice" Model

Trafford is not ready to be City's undisputed No. 1. The immediate risk is too high. The recommended strategy is to activate the £40M buy-back, but integrate him as a direct competitor to Stefan Ortega. This allows him to develop within an elite environment, gain experience in cup games, and address his weaknesses without the crushing pressure of being the first choice, securing a high-potential, homegrown asset for the long term.
